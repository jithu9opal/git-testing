kerala tourism

Kerala's tourism highlights in the districts of Ernakulam, Alappuzha, Idukki, and Wayanad offer a diverse and enchanting experience. **Ernakulam**, the commercial hub, is home to the vibrant Fort Kochi, historic Mattancherry Palace, and the iconic Chinese fishing nets. **Alappuzha**, known as the "Venice of the East," is famous for its serene backwaters, houseboat cruises, and the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race. **Idukki**, with its lush hills and wildlife, boasts attractions like the Idukki Arch Dam, Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, and the picturesque hill station of Munnar, known for its tea plantations. **Wayanad**, nestled in the Western Ghats, offers stunning landscapes, including Edakkal Caves, Chembra Peak, and tranquil waterfalls like Meenmutty, making it a haven for nature and adventure enthusiasts. Together, these districts showcase Kerala's natural beauty, cultural richness, and unique experiences.
