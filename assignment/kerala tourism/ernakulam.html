ERNAKULAM

a vibrant district in Kerala, is a blend of modernity and tradition, offering a variety of attractions for tourists. Here are some must-visit places in Ernakulam:

1. Fort Kochi

Chinese Fishing Nets: Iconic cantilevered fishing nets that are a symbol of Kochi. St. Francis Church: The oldest European church in India, where Vasco da Gama was originally buried. Santa Cruz Basilica: A stunning Gothic-style church known for its beautiful architecture.

2. Mattancherry

Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace): A historic palace with exquisite murals depicting scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata. Jew Town: A historic area known for its antique shops and the Paradesi Synagogue, the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth.

3. Marine Drive

h Scenic Walkway: A picturesque promenade along the backwaters, perfect for evening strolls and boat rides. Shopping and Dining: Numerous shops and restaurants offering local delicacies and souvenirs.

4. Bolghatty Palace

Historic Palace: Located on Bolghatty Island, this Dutch palace is now a heritage hotel with lush gardens and a golf course. Bolghatty Event Center: Hosts various cultural events and exhibitions.

5. Cherai Beach

Golden Sands: A serene beach located on Vypin Island, ideal for swimming and picnics. Backwaters: Offers a unique combination of beach and backwater experiences.

6. Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary

Bird Watching: A green oasis in the heart of the city, home to various species of migratory and resident birds. Nature Trails: Walking paths for nature enthusiasts.

7. Hill Palace Museum

Royal Artifacts: The largest archaeological museum in Kerala, showcasing artifacts from the Kochi royal family. Heritage Garden: Beautifully landscaped gardens surrounding the palace.
